Predicting Wind Turbine Failures Before They Happen

Wind turbine repairs are costly and disruptive, especially when failures come without warning. For this competition, our team built a system that spots problems early using sensor data that turbines already collect — no new hardware needed. We worked with data from 36 turbines across three wind farms in Portugal and Germany.

We built two detection methods and ran them side by side. One learned from past fault records to recognize warning signs. The other learned what "healthy" looks like, so it could flag anything unusual — even problems it had never seen before. Together, they power a three-level alert system (Watch, Alert, Action) that tells maintenance teams where to look and which sensor triggered the warning, cutting down unnecessary inspections by around 60%.

Redesigning Transit for a Growing Town

Cochrane is one of Alberta's fastest-growing towns, but its transit system hasn't kept up. With only 8 vehicles, 3 routes running every 60 minutes, and no way to reach Calgary by bus, it's difficult for residents without a car to get around. For comparison, Airdrie — roughly twice the size — runs 26 vehicles with routes every 30 minutes.

Our team used real movement data from fall 2025 to map how residents actually travel through the town. From that, we designed a new system with 5 routes covering all major neighbourhoods, running every 30 to 45 minutes. We also added a new direct route to Crowfoot C-Train Station in Calgary, giving residents one-transfer access to the University of Calgary, SAIT, and downtown. The plan is designed to scale as Cochrane grows toward its 2050 population targets.
